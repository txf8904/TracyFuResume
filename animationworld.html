Project Overview

YHZ 3D Animator is a showcase website to help a 3D animation student build a portfolio website for a potential job opportunity. The client is a new graduate student who does not have a lot of design projects and job experience.

Therefore, the client is looking for a showcase portfolio website that can best show the potential employer who she is and what she does. Also, the client wants the portfolio website to be user-friendly by providing clear navigation and a simple showcase for 3D animation works.

Understanding the user

Problem:

I conducted user interviews, which I then turned into empathy maps to better understand the user and needs. I discovered that many new-grad students faced the problem that limits of work can show to their potential employers. However, most good portfolio examples are people who have worked in the industry for many years with a lot of work. This caused a lack of confidence for new-grad students to have their own visual showcase.

Solution:

To solve the main problem, most of the works are based on college projects which did not have a big title/brand, I think it is important to have a focus point on the website. Most portfolio websites is showing all the work on the website without a unique point that grabs attention immediately. Also, most 3D animation portfolio websites show their works without a lot of information about the works which creates a simple feeling that meets the client's requirements.

The Design

Sitemaps:

My primary goal here is to create a simple and clear website. Also, fewer pages will show the focus points more easily and hide the disadvantage, lack of work, away.

Wireframes:

Next, I sketched out paper wireframes for the home screen on my website. I keep thinking about how to have a focus point when the visitors first view the websites. Meanwhile, show enough information that can show who the owner of the website is.

Low-fidelity Prototype:

Next, I connected all the screens and added quick access components for the main user flow.

Refining the design

Usability Study:

To better solve the client the problem, a face-to-face meeting was conducted.

After reviewing the basic feature and design for the website, the client brings up three considerations about the website.

First, the homepage focus point is not obvious. The work screenshot on the right and the introduction word and self avatar on the left have equal weight on the page.

Second, the work page does not have a focus point. Also, if the website owner has a small number of works, tiled the page with animation work is not a good way to show it.

Last, the client does not think that contact information needs a whole webpage to show.

Mockup:

Based on the feedback from the usability study, I made changes to improve the how mage. One of the changes I made was changing the size of the picture and font. By decreasing the size of the person's picture and the word “Hello”, the focus can move to the name and title.


To make the work page has a focus point, I changed a showcase into a slideshow on top which can highlight the work the owner wants the visitors to focus on. Also, this way will show fewer artworks.


Last, deleting the contact page and moving the contact information to the footer and about page.

High-fidelity Prototype:

My hi-fi prototype followed the same user flow as the lo-fi prototype with the design changes made after the usability study.

Final

Accessibility considerations:

There are two accessibilitiy considerations in the website. First, I avoid rich color theme, keep the website in a color blind friendly. Second, I used word as navigation instead of icons to help user easier to understand the website.

Takeaways:

Impact:
My clients shared that the design was clear and simple as she needs. A good design and focus point solve the problem of lack of work.

What I learned:
During the process, I found out that a small change in the component will have an impact on the overall design. Also, it is important to keep listening to your client’s needs and make changes to fit their needs.
